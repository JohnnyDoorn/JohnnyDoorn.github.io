# About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the [Psychological Methods](https://psyres.uva.nl/content/research-groups/programme-group-psychological-methods/programme-group-psychological-methods.html) department at the [University of Amsterdam](https://www.uva.nl/en). My research focuses on **Bayesian inference** and its application to psychological science, with a particular emphasis on developing and evaluating Bayesian hypothesis tests for rank-based data, mixed models, and other common psychological research designs.

## Research Interests

- Bayesian statistics and hypothesis testing
- Bayes factors for mixed models
- Nonparametric Bayesian methods (rank correlations, rank-based tests)
- Open science practices and reproducibility
- Statistical software development ([JASP](https://jasp-stats.org/))

## Book

I co-authored **Discovering Statistics Using JASP** with Andy Field. The book provides a comprehensive introduction to statistics using the free, open-source software JASP. Visit the companion website at [discoverjasp.com](https://discoverjasp.com) for additional resources, exercises, and data files.

## Teaching

I teach undergraduate statistics courses at the University of Amsterdam, including [Research Methods & Statistics](https://studiegids.uva.nl/xmlpages/page/2024-2025/zoek-vak/vak/117881) and [Scientific & Statistical Reasoning](https://studiegids.uva.nl/xmlpages/page/2024-2025/zoek-vak/vak/117879). I also develop materials for various statistics workshops. All my teaching materials are openly available in my [teaching-statistics GitHub repository](https://github.com/JohnnyDoorn/teaching-statistics).

## Software

I am a contributor to [JASP](https://jasp-stats.org/), a free and open-source statistics program that makes Bayesian and frequentist analyses accessible through an intuitive graphical interface.
